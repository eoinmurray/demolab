The leaky integrate-and-fire (LIF) model is the simplest spiking neuron worth taking seriously. Its membrane voltage decays toward a resting potential and is pushed around by injected current; when the voltage crosses a threshold the neuron emits a spike and resets.

The subthreshold dynamics are

$$
\tau_m \frac{dV}{dt} = -(V - V_\text{rest}) + R_m\, I,
$$

with a spike-and-reset rule

$$
V(t) \geq V_\text{th} \;\Longrightarrow\; \text{spike at } t,\quad V \leftarrow V_\text{reset}.
$$

The code integrates this with forward Euler at $\Delta t = 0.1\,\text{ms}$,

$$
V \leftarrow V + \frac{\Delta t}{\tau_m}\!\left[-(V - V_\text{rest}) + R_m\, I\right],
$$

using $\tau_m = 10\,\text{ms}$, $V_\text{rest} = -65\,\text{mV}$, $V_\text{reset} = -70\,\text{mV}$, $V_\text{th} = -50\,\text{mV}$, $R_m = 10\,\text{M}\Omega$, and tonic input $I = 2.5\,\text{nA}$.

With a steady tonic input current above threshold, the neuron fires periodically. Each upward sweep is the membrane charging through its RC time constant; each vertical line is a reset after a spike.

Connect 200 such neurons together with sparse, all-excitatory recurrence and noisy per-neuron bias currents, and the dynamics get more interesting. Each neuron $i$ obeys the same LIF dynamics, but with a total input current

$$
I_i(t) = I_i^\text{bias} + s_i(t), \qquad I_i^\text{bias} \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2),
$$

with $\mu = 2.2\,\text{nA}$ and $\sigma = 0.4\,\text{nA}$. The synaptic current $s_i$ decays exponentially between spikes and is kicked by incoming spikes,

$$
s_i \leftarrow s_i\, e^{-\Delta t / \tau_\text{syn}} + \!\!\sum_{j \,\in\, \text{spiked}} W_{ij},
$$

where $\tau_\text{syn} = 5\,\text{ms}$ and the weight matrix is $W_{ij} = w\, C_{ij}$ with $C_{ij} \sim \text{Bernoulli}(p_\text{conn})$, $w = 0.1$, $p_\text{conn} = 0.1$, and no self-connections. After each spike the neuron is held at $V_\text{reset}$ for a refractory period of $2\,\text{ms}$.

The raster below shows every spike from every neuron over half a second of simulated time; the panel underneath tracks the network's mean total input current (bias plus decaying synaptic input), with the shaded band showing one standard deviation across the population.
